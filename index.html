Getting started

Three steps between you and your first over

Step 1

Verify in DM

Tap the welcome button and press Start in the bot's DM — once. It unlocks squad tagging, bowling keyboards, and tournament rosters. Skip it and your deliveries never arrive.

Step 2

Open the menu

/startcricket drops a menu card in your group: Solo, Team, Tournaments, Help, Cancel. Only one card lives per chat — a fresh one retires the last.

Step 3

Play the duel

Bowler picks 0–6 in DM. Batter guesses in the group. Same number, OUT. Different number, that many runs. Everything else — stats, Rubies, tournaments — is built on this.

Game modes

Pick your format, we'll umpire

From a free-for-all with strangers to a full IPL-style league across groups — GullyCric scales from one over to a whole season.

Solo Mode

Every player bats their own innings

A free-for-all where everyone takes a turn batting while the rest of the group bowls. Best all-round performance wins the round.

  • /joinsolo to queue up, /leavesolo if you change your mind — the match auto-starts ~60s after the second player joins, or the host fires /forcestart.
  • Pick 3 or 6 balls per over. Join alone and you'll face the bot itself.
  • The number 0 is disabled entirely in Solo — every delivery and shot is 1–6.
  • A live standings card tracks runs, balls and wickets as the rounds rotate.
Team Mode

The flagship: two teams, one toss, a Super Over if it's tied

Captains, betting, uneven squads that auto-balance, and a full second innings chase. This is GullyCric at full stretch.

  • Claim Host → create team slots → players join with /join_team_a / /join_team_b during a ~95s window.
  • Host sets overs with /set_overs; the toss fires automatically and the winning captain picks bat or field.
  • Captains send batters and bowlers with /batting N and /bowling N — hosts can too.
  • Target is first-innings score + 1. Still tied after both innings? A one-over Super Over decides it.
  • Mid-match tools: /substitute (pre-innings, unlimited), /impact (max 2 per team), /add · /remove · /shift for the host.
Gameplay rules & quirks

The small print that decides close matches

Rotation

Strike & dismissals

Strike rotates on odd runs (1/3/5) and at the end of each over. Out for a duck on 0 runs; three wickets in a row is a hat-trick.

Team mode only

The tactical zero

A bowler may bowl one dead-ball 0 per over — a forced dot, no matter what the batter plays. Banned on the last 2 balls of an innings, and a batter can't block a hat-trick ball with it.

Fairness

Anti-spam limits

No bowler takes two overs back-to-back. The same delivery can't repeat more than twice running, or 3 times total in an over.

80 seconds

AFK protection

An idle bowler concedes +6 and gets rotated out; an idle batter is given out. Use /alert to ping teammates before the timer bites.

DM only

Bowling happens privately

When it's your turn, the bot DMs a number keyboard. No keyboard? Tap Bowl Delivery in the group and make sure you've verified.

/sledge

Commentary & banter

English or Hindi commentary via /changecom. Admins can toggle spicy sledging on or off with /sledgeon / /sledgeoff.

Betting & gifting

During the first innings only, back a side with /bet a <amount> or /bet b <amount>. Every stake goes into a shared pool and winners split it once the match ends. The window shuts the moment the first innings does.

Feeling generous? /gift sends Rubies to another player. Generosity is a virtue; bankruptcy is not.

Stats, leaderboards & rankings

Two leaderboard systems, and they measure different things

Personal

/userstat · /compare · /botstats

Your career record — runs, wickets, strike rate, milestones, Rubies balance — plus head-to-head comparisons and community-wide numbers.

/leaderboard

All-time Top-10s

Runs, Wickets, Sixes, Fours, Centuries, Fifties, Hat-Tricks, POTM, Catches, Hall of Fame, and the Tournament Hall of Fame archive. Forever boards — go make history.

/ranking

Weekly & all-time standings

This group's Orange/Purple Cap boards plus global weekly and all-time rankings, with your own position highlighted. Weekly resets every Monday.

Organisers can keep leaderboards clean with /excludestats, /includestats and /lockedstats — handy for bot and test accounts.

Tournaments

Full IPL-style leagues, automated end to end

Registered teams, group stages, a live points table with NRR, fixtures across multiple groups, playoffs, and champions immortalised in the Hall of Fame.

📊

Following along

/pointstable, /tourroster, /tourstats, /schedule (your own timezone via /mytimezone).

🛠️

Running one

/hosttournament walks organisers through naming the tour and registering teams, squad by squad.

🎛️

Control panel

/tournament opens Team Groups, Squads, Points Table, Status, Top Scorers, Fixtures and Schedule in one place.

🏆

Playoffs

/startplayoffs needs 4+ non-forfeited teams; seeds top 4 into Eliminator → Qualifiers → Final automatically.

Match results update the points table and NRR by themselves. Playoff brackets advance on their own. Orange/Purple Cap Rubies post automatically. Discipline tools — /forfeit, /pen, /deltourteam — stay in the organiser's back pocket for when a team goes quiet.

Roles & permissions

Everyone in the group has a job — some just have more buttons

Player

Anyone in the group. Joins matches, bats, bowls, bets, checks stats.

Captain

Chosen after the toss. Sends batters and bowlers, transfers captaincy, renames the team.

Host

Whoever claims host in setup. Controls the lobby, overs, rosters, force-start, timers — and everything a captain can.

Group Admin

Telegram admins of the group. Lock/unlock the bot, toggle sledging and cleanup, cancel matches, confirm /endcricket.

Organiser (Superman)

Trusted users granted full rights via /superman. Runs tournaments, authorises groups, sees the extended menu.

Core Developers

The three hardcoded master IDs. Only they grant or revoke Superman and use owner-level tools.

/endcricket needs 2 admin confirmations to abort a live match — no lone wolf nukes a game in progress.

Automation & housekeeping

A lot of the magic is invisible

01

Menu dedup

Only one main menu lives per chat — a fresh /startcricket retires the old card seconds after landing.

02

Self-cleaning messages

Warnings, /about cards, announcements and command echoes delete themselves after a short delay.

03

Post-match cleanup

Old match messages sweep away after a game — toggle per group with /cleanuppostmatch.

04

Welcome & verify

New members get a witty welcome and a verify button, auto-deleting in 60s — toggle with /welcomeplayers.

05

Flood control

A per-chat token bucket lets wicket bursts fire instantly while respecting Telegram's rate limits. The bot never crashes from it.

06

Crash-safe state

Live matches persist to disk — a redeploy or restart resumes cleanly instead of eating your match.
